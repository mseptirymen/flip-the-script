## Design Context

### Users
Pokemon TCG players who want to track their tournament results and better understand their performance. They use this both during tournaments (after rounds) and at home (to review and analyze their results). The core job is logging results to understand their record at different tournaments over time.

### Brand Personality
**Voice**: Warm, minimal, and calm. Friendly like Claude.ai — approachable without being cutesy.
**Tone**: Fun, competitive, organized, and professional. Serious about the data, friendly in presentation.
**3-word personality**: "Warm minimal competitor"

### Aesthetic Direction
Minimal and modern clean. Both light and dark themes already implemented. Avoid flashy or "cool" colors — the interface should feel like a refined tool, not a gaming dashboard. Think Claude.ai's warmth meets a professional results tracker.

**Reference**: Claude.ai — warm, friendly, inviting, minimal without being cold
**Anti-references**: ChatGPT, Gemini, Attio — these have a generic "AI product" look that should be avoided

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth through restraint** — Use soft neutral tones, never harsh contrasts. Warmth comes from generous spacing and friendly typography, not rounded corners or bright colors.
2. **Data clarity** — Tournament results are the hero. Make data easy to scan, compare, and understand at a glance.
3. **Professional but approachable** — Clean enough for serious players, friendly enough that it doesn't feel like enterprise software.
4. **Consistent rhythm** — Use the same spacing and layout patterns throughout so the interface feels predictable and calm.
5. **Subtle personality** — Pokemon is playful, but the interface shouldn't look like a toy. Let the content (Pokemon names, results) provide the personality, not the UI chrome.
